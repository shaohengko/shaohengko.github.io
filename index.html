About Me

My website is currently under re-construction. If you see this line, the re-construction is not done yet.

I am a PhD student at Duke CS, where I am advised by Kristin Stephens-Martinez.

After a winding road, I now work in Computing Education Research. My current research focuses on (i) students help-seeking behavior in large-scale computing curriculums, and (ii) non-programming-focused CS education.

Things I've worked on (i.e., the winding road) includes algorithms for fair redistricting, algorithms for efficient pricing, or algorithms for navigating in virtual reality. Rather than studying algorithms for X, I guess I'm now (partially) studying how to better prepare next generation computer scientists to study algorithms for X.

Before Duke, I worked at Academia Sinica. Prior to that, I got my BS and MS from National Taiwan University.
